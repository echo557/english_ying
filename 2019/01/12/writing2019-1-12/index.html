Represent Highest and Lowest Points in graphs

Highest:

  1. Verb
    peaked/culminated/climaxed/reach the peak/ hit the peak/touch the highest point/reach the vertex/reach the apex
  2. Noun
    a(/the)peak, a(/the)pinnacle, /a(/the) vertex, the highest point, an(/the) apex, a (/the) summit, a (/the) top,
    a(the) acme, a(/the) zenith

Lowest

  1. Verb
    touch the lowest point/ get the lowest point/reached the nadir
  2. Noun
    the lowest point, the lowest mark, bottommost point, rock bottom point, bottommost mark, nadir, the all-time low,
    the lowest level,the bottom, rock-bottom

Example:

  1. The price of the oil reached a peak amounting $20 in February and again touched the lowest point amounting only
    $10 in July.
  2. Students enrollment in foreign Universities and Colleges increased dramatically hitting a peak of over 20000 in 2004.
  3. The selling volume of the DVD hit a pick with 2 million copies sold in a month but after just three months it reached
    the bottom with only 20000 sold in a month.

Comparison in graphs

Similar: about/almost/nearly/roughly/approximately/about/just about/very nearly
Just over: just above/just over/just bigger/just beyond/just across
Just short: just below/just beneath/just sort/ just under/just a little
Much more:well above/well beyond/well across/well over
much less: well below/well under/well short/well beneath

Example:

  1. The number of high-level women executives is well beneath than the number of male executives in this
    organization, where approximately 2000 people work in executive levels.
  2. About 1000 people died in the highway car accident in 2003 which is well above than the statistics of
    all other years.
  3. The number of domestic violence cases was just below 500 in March which is just a little over thn the
    previous months.
  4. The average rainfall in London in 2014 was just above than the average of two other cities.
  5. The salaries of male executives in three out of four companies were well above than the salaries of
    female executives in 1998.

Make Comparison and Contrast

Similarly, In a similar fashion, In the same way, Same as, As much as, Meanwhile
However, On the contrary, on the other hand, in contrast

One Syllable
cheap>> cheaper>> cheapest| large>>larger>>largest

Exceptions:
good>> better>>best | bad>>worse>>worse
Example:

  1. The largest proportion of water was used in the agriculture sector in most of the Asian countries while the
    European countries used the highest percentage of water for industrial purposes.
  2. The price of the book in store A is cheaper than the price of store B.
  3. The temperature decreased further and that made the weather condition worse.

Two Syllables
pretty >> Prettier >> prettiest | happy >> happier >> happiest

Example:
The overall production level of this company made the authority happier as it was doubled in the last quarter of
the year.

striking >> more striking >> most striking | common >> more common >> most common

Three or more Syllables
attractive >> more attractive >> most attractive || profitable >> more profitable >> most profitable
